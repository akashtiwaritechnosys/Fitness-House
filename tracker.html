                    Food & Nutrition Guide

                    Discover the best foods for your fitness goals. Every meal is an opportunity to build, recover, and perform at your best.

                    Veg
                    Muscle Gain

                    Paneer (Cottage Cheese)

                    A high-protein Indian staple made from fresh cow or buffalo milk. Rich in casein protein, it digests slowly making it ideal for muscle recovery and sustained satiety throughout the day.

                    265 kcal/ 100g
                    18gProtein
                    3.4gCarbs
                    20gFat
                    0gFiber
                    Best eaten grilled or raw. Avoid deep frying to keep it lean.
                    Veg
                    Muscle + Fat Loss

                    Lentils (Dal)

                    A powerhouse legume rich in plant-based protein and complex carbohydrates. High fiber content promotes gut health, controls blood sugar, and keeps you feeling full longer — perfect for both bulk and cut phases.

                    116 kcal/ 100g cooked
                    9gProtein
                    20gCarbs
                    0.4gFat
                    8gFiber
                    Pair with rice for a complete amino acid profile. Add turmeric for anti-inflammatory benefits.
                    Veg
                    Energy + Fat Loss

                    Rolled Oats

                    The ultimate pre-workout breakfast staple. Oats provide slow-releasing beta-glucan carbohydrates that fuel sustained energy, lower cholesterol, and prevent mid-workout energy crashes.

                    389 kcal/ 100g dry
                    17gProtein
                    66gCarbs
                    7gFat
                    11gFiber
                    Mix with banana and peanut butter for a 500+ kcal pre-workout power bowl.
                    Veg
                    Recovery

                    Greek Yogurt (Hung Curd)

                    Strained yogurt packed with live probiotics and high-quality protein. The perfect post-workout snack to kickstart muscle repair, support gut microbiome, and replenish glycogen stores.

                    97 kcal/ 100g
                    10gProtein
                    4gCarbs
                    5gFat
                    0gFiber
                    Add honey and mixed berries for antioxidants. Ideal 30 mins post-workout.
                    Veg
                    Muscle + Fat Loss

                    Chickpeas (Chana)

                    A nutritional powerhouse used across Indian cuisine. Chickpeas deliver sustained energy, significant plant protein, and a massive dose of dietary fiber that supports weight management and digestive health.

                    164 kcal/ 100g cooked
                    9gProtein
                    27gCarbs
                    2.6gFat
                    8gFiber
                    Roasted chana is a great pre-gym snack. Add to salads for a protein boost.
                    Veg
                    Energy

                    Banana

                    Nature's energy bar. Bananas are loaded with fast-acting carbohydrates, potassium, and vitamin B6. They prevent muscle cramps during training and rapidly replenish glycogen stores post-workout.

                    89 kcal/ 100g
                    1.1gProtein
                    23gCarbs
                    0.3gFat
                    2.6gFiber
                    Eat 1 banana 30 mins before training for a natural energy surge. Pair with peanut butter for lasting fuel.
                    Non-Veg
                    Muscle Gain

                    Chicken Breast

                    The gold standard of bodybuilding nutrition. Skinless chicken breast is incredibly lean with an extremely high protein-to-calorie ratio. It contains all 9 essential amino acids needed for muscle protein synthesis.

                    165 kcal/ 100g
                    31gProtein
                    0gCarbs
                    3.6gFat
                    0gFiber
                    Grill or bake. Marinate in curd + spices for flavor without extra calories.
                    Non-Veg
                    Muscle + Recovery

                    Whole Eggs

                    Calorie for calorie, one of the most nutritious foods on earth. Eggs contain choline for brain health, leucine for muscle synthesis, and healthy fats in the yolk that support testosterone production.

                    155 kcal/ 100g
                    13gProtein
                    1.1gCarbs
                    11gFat
                    0gFiber
                    3 whole eggs + 3 egg whites is the perfect muscle-building breakfast. Boiled or poached is best.
                    Non-Veg
                    Recovery

                    Salmon / Rohu Fish

                    A supreme source of omega-3 fatty acids (EPA & DHA) that combat exercise-induced inflammation, accelerate muscle recovery, and support cardiovascular health. Also rich in vitamin D and selenium.

                    208 kcal/ 100g
                    20gProtein
                    0gCarbs
                    13gFat
                    0gFiber
                    Eat 2–3 times a week. Steam or bake to preserve omega-3 content. Avoid deep frying.
                    Non-Veg
                    Fat Loss

                    Tuna (Canned / Fresh)

                    An elite cutting food — extremely high protein with almost zero fat and carbohydrates. Tuna is loaded with selenium, niacin, and B12 that support metabolism and energy production during calorie deficits.

                    132 kcal/ 100g
                    28gProtein
                    0gCarbs
                    1.3gFat
                    0gFiber
                    Choose tuna in water, not oil. Mix with Greek yogurt instead of mayo to keep it lean.
                    Non-Veg
                    Muscle Gain

                    Chicken Thigh (With Skin)

                    Juicier and more flavourful than breast, chicken thighs offer a higher calorie count with more zinc and iron. Ideal during bulking phases where caloric surplus is needed for serious muscle building.

                    209 kcal/ 100g
                    26gProtein
                    0gCarbs
                    11gFat
                    0gFiber
                    Remove skin before eating during cut phases. Great in stir-fry with vegetables.
                    Non-Veg
                    Recovery

                    Whey Protein Shake

                    The fastest-absorbing protein available, derived from milk. Whey triggers rapid muscle protein synthesis post-workout, contains leucine for anabolic signaling, and is perfect when whole-food protein intake falls short.

                    120 kcal/ 1 scoop (30g)
                    24gProtein
                    3gCarbs
                    2gFat
                    0gFiber
                    Consume within 30 mins post-workout. Mix with milk for extra protein and casein for prolonged recovery.
